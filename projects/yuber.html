Yuber Case Study

Tools: Figma, Procreate


Problem

The challenge: Yale students need affordable airport transportation. While ride-sharing is the most cost-effective solution, the current system - a public Facebook group - is problematic. Posts get buried, spam is common, and finding compatible schedules is tedious.











Previous solutions haven't quite hit the mark:
- Ride Buddies offers Yale-specific privacy but suffers from poor design and low adoption
- Hopp has a better interface but lacks Yale-specific features, making actual ride matching difficult















Solution

I designed Yale RideShare, an app that combines familiar interface patterns from various successful platforms:

- Booking mechanics inspired by Uber and Lyft
- Matching features similar to dating apps
- A clean, organized feed that improves upon the Facebook group experience

The app prioritizes both privacy and usability, creating a Yale-specific platform that makes ride coordination simple and secure.













Design Process

I began in Procreate, sketching initial concepts and user flows to explore different approaches to ride matching and scheduling. Moving to Figma, I developed these ideas into a high-fidelity prototype that demonstrates the complete user experience.
