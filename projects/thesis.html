Yale Undergraduate Thesis

Tools: Figma, Procreate, Premiere Pro, Adobe InDesign


My magnum opus (so far).

For my senior thesis in Digital Studies at Yale, I created an immersive multimedia exhibition exploring how surveillance culture reshapes our understanding of authenticity, selfhood, and privacy in the Digital Age. The project's central aim was to make invisible forms of everyday surveillance viscerally apparent to viewers.

I designed an intentionally overwhelming environment that manifests the weight of constant observation - the unseen gazes that shape our daily lives. The installation comprised five distinct pieces, each examining a different facet of surveillance:
- A sculptural work
- An animated piece
- A website/film experience
- A zine publication
- A graphic design component


Each element was supported by extensive scholarly research and theoretical framework, detailed in my accompanying thesis paper. The entire exhibition has been adapted into a virtual experience, allowing for broader access to this exploration of surveillance's pervasive influence.
